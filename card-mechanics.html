Wizards of the Coast has been making cards for the game for over 20 years. Because of this they have a lot of different card mechanics and lots of rulesas such this will only go over some of the more common ones

Different card mechanics

Trample: is an ability that lets an attacking creatures damage go through the creature it is blocking if its power exeeds that creatures toughness/health

Vigilance: lets a creature attack without tapping this does not mean the creature can attack twice but if it has another ability that could be used or it could be saved for blocking

Defender: Is simple in that a creature with defender cannot , many wall type cards use Defender

Haste: Lets a creature attack the turn it is played

Flash: Enables a card to be played during the opponents turn like an instant card

Phases

A turn in magic has multiple phases in which actions can be done

  1. Begining phase
  2. Pre combat main phase
  3. combat phase
  4. Post combat main phase
  5. Ending phase

The begining phase is made up of three parts the untap stap where you untap tapped permanents such as land creatures artifacts. Then comes the upkeep step, some cards require you to pay an upkeep and if you dont the card will usually have to be sacraficed meaning it has to go into your graveyard. Lastly is the draw step where a player draws their card

The pre combat main phase is the phase where most actions can be done sorcerys can only be cast in the pre or post main, phase some cards abilities will function like this as well. This is where you would play a land or a creature

The combat phase is where a player declares what creatures are attacking and which player they are attacking the player being attacked can then declare blockers or what their creatures are going to block. Instants can also be cast during this phase even if it isnt your turn

After combat is finished and damage has been dealt comes the post main phase which functions identically to the pre main phase other than the fact that combat has taken plase

The Ending phase has two steps the end step and cleanup step. The end step is where certatin cards abilities activate while the cleanup ste p is where one would discard their cards if they exeeded the maximum hand size.
