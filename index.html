How to play Magic The Gathering

Magic the gathering is a huge cardgame made by Wizards of the coast in which players battle eachother

The core mechanic feature 5 different colors each with their own stratedgy a player can use as many or as little colors as they want

The game is somewhat complicated but after a few games a player can come to understand most of the rules

Magic the gathering is a very old card game with many rules. As such it can be difficult for new people to learn.

Some Basic rules

A standard game will have players start at 20 life and cut their deck show the card they pull and the player with the highest cost card gets to decide the turn order the person who goes first does not draw on the first turn

Cards cost lands/mana you can play one land each turn normally some cards can get around this like searching the deck for a basic land card and putting into play tapped

Tapping a card means its action has been used, a land would be tapped in order to pay a cards mana cost or a creature would have to tap to attack

The objective of the game is to make your opponent lose his or her total life and reduce it to zero. Some cards may allow someone to win the game wi thout reducing your opponents life but these are rare and usually hard to activate

Players have a max hand size of seven cards; if they exeed this size they have to discard a card.

Cards hava a mana cost lands are the main way of generating mana

There are 6 different colors in magic red, blue, green, white, and black, each color has a different stratedgy

Creatures can only attack once per turn.

Deck building

A standard deck size is 60 cards. you cant have more than four of the same card, Basic lands are excluded from this rule.

Some cards will have a legendary rule. Only one of that legendary cards can exist on the battlefield/board

Card types

Lands are the most important card type as the game is impossible with out lands or some way of generating mana.

Lands generate mana depending on their color some can let the player choose the kind of mana it creates.

Creatures cards have a creature type and are capable of attacking and blocking.

A creature has to tap to attack unless it has vigilance it does not need to tap. This creature is better suited for blocking due to its high toughness and low power.

Artifacts have many different abilities and can adapt to fit the deck or players can choose to focus artifacts instead of a color.

Sorcery is another card type that can be cast for an immediate effect but only during the casters turn

Instant are spells much like sorceries but can be cast even during your opponents turn provided you can pay the mana cost

Enchantments provide a continuing affect after being played and can only be destroyed by specific spells or abilities

Auras are a more specific enchantment and usually attach themselves to a specific creature and only affect the attached creature

Planewalkers are a very special card type and can provide powerful effects. They have their own health bar and have abilities that can add or subtract their health

Equipment are a more specific artifact. A player has to pay to play it and then pay the equipment cost to attach it to a creature and while the creature may die the equipment stays
